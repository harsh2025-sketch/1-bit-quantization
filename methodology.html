Abstract

Recent advancements in extreme low-bit serialization have demonstrated the viability of 1.58-bit ternary large language models for minimizing computational and memory footprints. However, standard low-bit architectures introduce a hypothesized degradation mechanism we term Boundary Representation Collapse at the embedding interfaces, suffer from signal amplitude decay at extreme depths, and fail to address the memory asymmetry wherein continuous 16-bit Key-Value (KV) cache tensors outgrow weight parameters at extended context scales. This paper introduces a hardware-bounded transformer framework to investigate these scaling anomalies.
